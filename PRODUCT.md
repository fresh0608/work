# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Operations staff who use internal tools for private-domain operations, ad delivery, lead assignment, lead follow-up, and AI-assisted customer workflows. They are answering quickly during work and should not need product-management vocabulary to understand the survey.

## Product Purpose

Collect concise, concrete feedback about which internal product functions help daily operations, which parts are hard to use, and which improvement should be prioritized. Operator-facing copy should gather usage evidence; product-quality analysis belongs in the admin dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Clear, calm, work-focused.

## Anti-references

Do not expose internal evaluation language such as product design ability, design reliability, PM capability, architecture rigor, or personal assessment to operators. Avoid decorative UI, heavy cards, mixed typography, vague questions, and anything that feels like an exam or performance review.

## Design Principles

- Ask in operator language, analyze in product language behind the scenes.
- Make the next action obvious at the point where the user needs it.
- Keep hierarchy quiet and consistent: one primary action, clear section titles, stable controls.
- Prefer concrete scenarios over abstract judgments.
- Reduce cognitive load by showing one function and one task at a time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use readable system fonts, visible labels, keyboard-operable controls, 44px minimum touch targets, clear focus states, and sufficient contrast for all text and form controls.
